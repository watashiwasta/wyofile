California Boy Once Banned for Trump Hat Now Draws Snake Art to Support Veterans

9-year-old Logan Autry, once banned from wearing a Trump hat to school, is now auctioning his latest drawing to support military veterans through Give Us Paws.
by Micah Richardson May 6, 2025 13:50 PM ESTJanuary 20, 2025
Share this:

Now 9 years old, Logan has created a new drawing that’s already stirring interest online. The simple, hand-drawn image features a curled snake stylized with a Bitcoin dollar sign, and is being auctioned off to raise money for a cause close to the young artist’s heart: helping American veterans.

Story

Three days into the Wyoming legislative session and already we’ve heard testimony and seen bill titles that claim to be designed to “keep women safe or protected.” I’m concerned that these bills and the testimony given by a growing number of people are less about my well-being, and more about using women’s “safety” as a tool for political manipulation — bills like “What is a Woman Act” and “Protecting Women’s Safety in Public Spaces.”

The drawing is part of a one-item online auction titled “Auction for a Cause: Supporting America’s Veterans.” According to the site, 100% of the proceeds will be donated to Give Us Paws, a Texas-based nonprofit that trains service dogs for veterans suffering from PTSD, traumatic brain injuries, and mobility impairments.

The image — drawn in bold, childlike strokes — features a coiled snake resembling the Gadsden flag symbol, with a Bitcoin-inspired twist. Though simple, the drawing is already generating buzz on social media, both for its political symbolism and the backstory behind the young artist.

Independent news for independent thinkers

The image — drawn in bold, childlike strokes — features a coiled snake resembling the Gadsden flag symbol, with a Bitcoin-inspired twist. Though simple, the drawing is already generating buzz on social media, both for its political symbolism and the backstory behind the young artist.

Logan first caught public attention during the 2016 election cycle when his school asked him not to wear his signature “Make America Great Again” cap, citing classroom disruption. The story quickly went viral, turning Logan into a miniature icon among some conservative circles.

Now, he’s channeling that same energy into charitable action.

“By placing a bid, you’re not just acquiring a unique piece,” the auction site reads. “You’re helping a veteran reclaim their peace, dignity, and independence.”

I appreciate the discussions and work being done during this legislative session to support education, health care access, economic opportunity and community. I ask our Wyoming Legislature to focus on the issues at hand and stop using women’s safety as an excuse to pass bills that cannot stand on merit.

The auction closes in less than 24 hours. Bids are being accepted via email only.
